Catalog of 500+ no-code and low-code tools across automation, app builders, internal tools, and integration platforms.

What you'd use this for

  1. "Pick the right no-code tool" decision support — Filter by use-case (automation 119, app-builder 115, website 60, chatbot 27, internal-tools 19) + pricing + codeType (no-code vs low-code). The dataset replaces hours of "Best X for Y 2026" blog reading.
  2. Indie founder's MVP-stack picker — A non-technical founder wants to ship an MVP. Filter by app-builder (115 platforms — Bubble, Glide, Adalo, Softr, etc.) + freemium pricing → an actionable shortlist of platforms to evaluate over a weekend.
  3. Internal automation team's tool catalog — **119 automation platforms** — Zapier, Make, n8n, Pipedream, Tray, plus 114 niche-specific automation tools. An ops team builds an internal "approved automation tool" list filtered by integration breadth and pricing.
  4. AI no-code shopping list — **34 AI/ML no-code platforms** — from voiceflow.com (chatbot) to GPT-builder UIs to no-code RAG. Citizen developers picking AI tools without engineering get a structured catalog.
  5. Low-code-replacing-engineering case studies — The dataset's codeType=low-code filter (vs. no-code) gives the "we hire 1 engineer + use low-code" persona a structured catalog. Mendix, OutSystems, PowerApps category.
  6. Lead-gen for no-code-adjacent SaaS / agencies — The 500+ vendors are also commercial SaaS targets — agencies serving the no-code customer base, integration vendors, no-code training products. The dataset is the addressable-vendor list.
